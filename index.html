About Me

Hello! I'm an aspiring embedded systems engineer with a strong interest in robotics. I'm currently pursuing my Bachelor's degree in Mechatronics Engineering with a focus on embedded systems and control theory at University of Port-Harcourt, Rivers-State, Nigeria.

My journey into embedded systems began when I first programmed an Arduino to control LED patterns during a college workshop. That simple project sparked my fascination with the intersection of hardware and software, leading me to explore more complex projects involving sensors, actuators, and real-time control systems.

During my academic career, I've had the opportunity to work on several robotics projects, from line-following robots to more advanced systems using computer vision. These experiences have strengthened my skills in C/C++ programming, PCB design, and understanding the intricacies of microcontroller-based systems.

I'm currently seeking an internship or entry-level position where I can apply my technical knowledge and continue learning in a professional environment. I'm particularly interested in roles focused on embedded firmware development, control systems, or robotics.

An autonomous robot designed to follow a black line on a white surface using infrared sensors.

Features:

  • - Uses an array of 5 IR sensors for precise line detection
  • - Implements PID control algorithm for smooth tracking and minimal oscillation
  • - Designed custom PCB for sensor array and motor control
  • - 3D printed chassis for optimal sensor placement
  • - Capable of handling curves and intersections

Technical challenges included calibrating the sensors for different lighting conditions and tuning the PID constants for optimal performance across various track designs
